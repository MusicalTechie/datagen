If the goal was to set up a basic Oracle Fusion/Cloud SCM (supply chain management) environment to perform basic planning activities (S&OP, Demand Planning, Supply Planning, and Replenishment planning), what is the likely minimum amount of information required?  Possible examples for consideration are shown following this paragraph; assess and improve as appropriate.

REQUIRED:
- at least one "legal entity"
- at least one "business unit"
- at least one "calendar" and knowledge of monthly, weekly, or daily periods for each type of plan
- at least one "item"
- at least one "organization"
- at least one "customer"
- at least one "item catalog" (which acts as a product hierarchy)
- at least one "assignment set"
- at least one forecast-capable combination per item (e.g. item-organization-customer or item-organization or item-customer).
- at least 24 periods (and likely 24 calendar months regardless) of historical sales information ("bookings history" or "sales history")
- at least 3 periods periods of open orders in the past and 3 to 6 periods of open orders in the future.
- at least one FBDI file to load items
- at least one FBDI file to load locations
- at least one FBDI file to load bookings-history and/or shipment-history
- at least one FBDI file to open sales orders
- at least one "S&OP plan" with an associated "calendar" and "assignment set"
- at least one "demand plan" with an associated "calendar" and "assignment set"
- at least one "supply plan" with an associated "calendar" and "assignment set"
- at least one "replenishment plan" with an associated "calendar" and "assignment set"
- at least one "page layout" for each type of plan
- at least one "table" for each type of plan (which is assigned to a "page layout" and appears as a menu tab in the layout)


OPTIONAL:
- exception sets
- ODBI or BIP report
- lots and lot-control
- subinventories (per organization)
- segments (for planning)
- custom measures
- custom dimensions


QUESTIONS:
- Should the "locations" available be considered "organizations" or "customers" or should there be both separately?
- To reasonably model a real company, would MANY items with ONE organization with MANY customers be sufficient?
- what else may be REQUIRED vs OPTIONAL?
